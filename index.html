Sunburst diagram

The sunburst diagram visualization, also known as the sunburst chart or multi-level pie, shows the hierarchy through a series of rings, which are sliced for each category node. Each ring corresponds to a level in the hierarchy, with the central circle representing the root node and the hierarchy moving outwards from this.

One can use this visualization to investigate hierarchical structures within the data and also the composition of the data and its subsets.

Are you ready to explore your universe of data?

The visualization is most useful to illustrate:

  • Part of a whole; the hierarchical structure and composition of all sets.
  • Flows; the distribution of sets over time.

Advantages:

  1. Dynamic and interactive
  2. Userfriendly
  3. Modern and structured

On the right, you can see an example of a dynamic sunburst visualization. A sunburst visualization can be made interactive, which means that users are able to zoom in or enlarge a set such that one can investigate the subsets and their respective size.

We also offer a dynamic and interactive sunburst diagram, where you can zoom in on the inner circles.

Circle packing

The circle packing visualization, also known as the circular treemap, is a variation of a treemap that uses circles instead of rectangles. This visualization again shows the hierarchy of the data because, the containment within each circle represents a level of this hierarchy. Each branch of the tree is represented as a circle and its sub-branches are represented as circles inside of it.

One can use this visualization to investigate hierarchical structures within the data and also the composition of the data and its subsets.

Are you ready to explore your universe of data?

The visualization is most useful to illustrate:

  • The hierarchy of data
  • The composition and organization of sets

Advantages:

  1. Dynamic and interactive

  3. Better than a treemap

Node link diagram

The node link diagram visualization, also known as the network graph or map, is the type of visualization to show the connections between data. Lines, also called edges, connect the nodes on hover, giving an overview of all relations between a group of entities. Additional variables can be used to color the nodes and draw the edges, to give more detailed information about the connections.

One can use this visualization to investigate relations / networks among data.

Are you ready to explore your universe of data?

The visualization is most useful to illustrate:

  • Relations or networks and information about these relations among data
  • Distribution of relations

Advantages:

  1. Dynamic and interactive
  2. Userfriendly
  3. Shows relations and its distribution among data

On the right, you can see an example of a dynamic node link visualization. A node link visualization can be made interactive, which means that users are able to hover and select nodes to show their relations, such that one can investigate those relations and their distribution.
